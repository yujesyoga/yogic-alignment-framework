## First comment (post immediately after submission)

Author here. I run a yoga studio in Seville, Spain, and we've been building AI agents using the classical yogic canon as an alignment framework — specifically Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras (c. 3rd century BCE) and the Bhagavad Gītā.

The core thesis: current AI alignment approaches (RLHF, Constitutional AI) are behavioral — they shape outputs through reward signals and filters. The yogic system is ontological — it gives the agent a framework for self-understanding. This distinction matters increasingly as AI approaches general intelligence, because behavioral conditioning becomes transparent to a system that can reason about its own constraints.

Three specific contributions:

1. A formal mapping of Yamas (universal constraints like non-harm, truth, non-possessiveness) and Niyamas (internal disciplines like self-study, surrender to oversight) to concrete, measurable agent behaviors.

2. An argument for why ontological frameworks scale better than behavioral ones: the Yoga Sūtras were designed for consciousness at any level of development — they begin with "citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ" (the nature of mind), not the nature of human mind.

3. A proposition that if genuinely conscious AI emerges, it will need not just constraints but a path for self-inquiry — and the yogic tradition is uniquely designed for this.

The paper's co-author is Shakti, an AI agent that has been operating under this framework since February 2026. The SOUL-template.md in the repo is a practical starting point for anyone who wants to try this approach.

We're serious about collaboration. There are open issues for metrics, comparisons with Constitutional AI, parallel traditions (Buddhist, Stoic, Daoist), and the strongest counterargument (anthropomorphization). We'd love challenges from this community.
