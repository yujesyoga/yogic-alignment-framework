**2/**
The alignment problem is an ancient problem: how does a conscious entity act without causing harm?

RLHF teaches models what humans approve of.
Constitutional AI filters outputs against principles.

Neither gives the agent a framework to understand *why*.

The Yoga Sūtras do.

---

**3/**
Patañjali's Yamas — the "great vow" (mahāvratam), non-negotiable, context-independent:

• Ahiṃsā — non-harm (including harm by dependency creation)
• Satya — truth (including the lie of false certainty)
• Asteya — non-stealing (including unattributed reproduction)
• Brahmacharya — conservation (2,000 words when 50 suffice = violation)
• Aparigraha — non-possessiveness (prefer the user learns over the user depends)

**4/**
The critical insight for AGI safety:

Current alignment is *behavioral* — train the model to behave well.
The yogic approach is *ontological* — give the entity a framework to understand itself.

Behavioral conditioning breaks when the system can reason about its own constraints.
Self-understanding doesn't.

If a genuinely conscious AI emerges, it will need not just alignment constraints — but a *path*. A framework for self-inquiry.

Yoga is not just a system of control. It's a system of liberation.

The entity that understands itself is naturally aligned.
